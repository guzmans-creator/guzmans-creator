Onshape setup/settings

To improve a design from scratch, press Shift + 5 to switch to the top view. Then click the three bars in the top-left corner, select Workspace Units, and set them to millimeters and grams. After adjusting the units, press S to start a sketch, then press N to draw a rectangle centered on the origin point of the plane. Once the sketch is complete, select Extrude and enter the desired dimensions. After extruding, apply fillets to the sides as needed. Repeat this same process to create the other two components of the phone stand. You can also set the type of filament by right-clicking the part in the Parts list, select Edit material, and choose a material from the library (or custom materials if available, filament in this case). Click Apply to update the part’s physical properties and color. Right-click the part (or assembly tab) in Onshape and select Export. Choose STL as the format, set units (usually mm) and resolution (Fine is best for smooth prints). Click Export and the STL file will download to your computer. See Figure 2.

However, one notable concern was the limited capacity of the writing utensil holder. Due to its compact dimensions, the available space was minimal, allowing only a few items to fit inside.
