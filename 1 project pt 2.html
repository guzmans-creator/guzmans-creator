How To Use Prusa Slicer Software

Prusa Slicer is an open-source, free program for 3D printing. It is the software that converts a 3D model, such as an STL or 3MF file, into G-code instructions that a 3D printer can comprehend. The nozzle in the X, Y, and Z locations will move in accordance with these directions. Once the software is downloaded one can make modifications to the print settings. For instance, make sure to set the printer to Prusa MINI+, the filament to PLA, and the print quality to 0.20mm for this 3D name tag design. After that, download a 3MF file of the name tag picture and drag it into PrusaSlicer. The image showed up as a shadow of the name tag, and once released, it will be ready for editing. Click on the "Text - First Name" and "Text - Last Name" buttons in the right-hand corner of the screen to change the text on the name tag, then enter your name. See figure 1.Once this step is completed download the 3D design name tag onto a hard drive which saves as “.gcode…”. See figure 1.2. The printer will then begin printing! See Figure 1.3.

Prusa Slicer MINI+

When it is time to load the Prusa MINI, turn on the 3D printer using the switch located near the USB outlet on the right side of the device. After turning on the printer, you will be able to add PLA filament to the nozzle. Navigate to "Filament" on the display settings' right-hand side, choose "load filament," choose "PLA," and fill the nozzle with filament. See Figure 2. For simpler insertion, snip any fresh filament at a 45-degree angle. You will feel a slight tug which indicates that the filament loading was successful, see the nozzle completely inserted with filament. See figure 2.1.

Personal Analysis

In the end, my own 3D name tag design was severely flawed. See Figure 3. I hypothesize that the reason the name tag turned out that way was because the nozzle wasn't completely primed with the molten plastic. This could have been because the filament was slow or constrained during the color swap between the light blue and pink filaments. The primer lining appeared thin, fractured, and generally unfinished as a result of this. To guarantee the right result, I made sure to discharge and reprime the nozzle for the second name tag.
